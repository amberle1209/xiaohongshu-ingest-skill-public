# WeChat and other hosted posts

## WeChat public articles

Use a user-authorized feed, export, or integration when one exists. Do not claim a browser-style fetch succeeded merely because a page returned an interstitial or partial preview. If the article body is blocked, report the block and offer to archive a user-provided copy.

## Other hosted posts

Use a bounded HTTP fetch only for public `http(s)` URLs. Follow the configured redirect and size limits, then verify that the final URL remains public and that the response is an expected text or media type.

Extract the substantive body, title, author/date when directly available, and canonical URL. If a page is script-rendered, blocked, or only exposes metadata, create a partial preview record rather than guessing at the missing body.

## Source discipline

- Attribute claims to the source, especially metrics, testimonials, and screenshots.
- Keep copied source material separate from interpretation.
- Do not reproduce private, paywalled, or access-controlled content outside the user-authorized local archive.
